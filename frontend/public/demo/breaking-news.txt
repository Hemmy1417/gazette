MAYOR REFUSES TO RESIGN: Ramirez vows to fight charges in court

The mayor of Riverbrook, Elena Ramirez, said today she will not step down and
will contest every allegation, following a six-month investigation into the
awarding of city construction contracts to firms tied to her family.

"I have nothing to hide and I will not be pushed out of an office the people
elected me to hold," Ramirez said at a defiant news conference. "I will meet
these charges in court, and I will meet them head on." She took questions for
nearly an hour.

Ramirez's attorneys have already filed motions to dismiss the case, arguing
prosecutorial overreach. The state attorney general's office declined to
comment on the pending litigation but said the investigation remains active.
No trial date has been set.
